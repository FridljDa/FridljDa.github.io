As described in my previous post on [Cursor's model selection strategy](https://www.cursor.com/blog/cursor-model-selection-strategy),
I use different Models for different tasks in Cursor. At the time of writing this post:
- Default Planning model: `GPT-5.3 Codex` or `Composer 2`
    - In my experience, `Composer 2` is very concise in the sense that it takes few tokens to achieve the same result. See https://cursor.com/blog/composer-2 for more details
- Planning model when I am feeling like premium: `GPT-5.4` or `Claude 4.6 Sonnet`
    - I have noticed that  `Claude 4.6 Sonnet` is more likely to challenge my ideas, which I appreciate
- Plan execution model: `Gemini 3 Flash`

Despite this cost optimization strategy, Cursor costs have been steadily increasing over the past few months. In this post, I downloaded my usage data from the [Cursor dashboard](https://cursor.com/dashboard/usage) and analyzed it to understand the cost drivers.
